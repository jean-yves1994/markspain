Alpharetta Team


Whether you’re looking to buy or sell a home in Alpharetta, Georgia, you’re in a great position. A large city in the northern portion of Fulton County, Alpharetta is home to almost 70,000 people. With plenty of options for home buyers to choose from, Alpharetta real estate includes an abundance of condos, townhomes, new constructions, and luxury single-family residences. Located east of Woodstock, just south of Milton and northeast of Roswell, Alpharetta was once home to Cherokee Indians, and is built around a natural spring that once served as a major trading post for the city. Buying and selling homes in Alpharetta has been a special focus for the experts at David Marshall Team for several years, as it remains one of the most desirable Atlanta suburbs.

Alpharetta, Georgia is a vibrant southern city with a gorgeous downtown that offers a blend of outstanding schools, excellent job opportunities, unrivaled shopping and a variety of cultural activities and events. It continues to grow, and as one of the hottest markets in both commercial and residential real estate, Alpharetta has something exciting for everyone. The recent development of the Avalon—an 86-acre community that features a hotel, shopping district, restaurants, theaters, a conference center, residences and more—has only accelerated the growth and modernization of Alpharetta. With over 15,000 households, Alpharetta is home to all sorts of residents, the majority of whom are between the age of 18 and 30, with a median age of 33.

Alpharetta Team

Alpharetta real estate embodies numerous different types of communities. Leaning more toward upscale listings, Alpharetta offers southern charm with a touch of class, anything from excellent starter homes to elegant luxury estates. A majority of Alpharetta listings feature traditional and European architectural design elements and flavors, open concept interiors, split-level layouts, and commanding porches. Additionally, Alpharetta real estate is surrounded by natural beauty, as dense, mature trees separate many of the lots, adding both beauty and privacy. Roughly 54 percent of the households are home to a married couple, and around 40 percent of households are home to at least one child under the age of 18.

Buy a Home In Alpharetta

If you’re looking to buy a home in Alpharetta, be prepared to enter a competitive real estate market. With several extremely appealing amenities and attractions, Alpharetta has sustained substantial growth over the last decade. On average, Alpharetta homes sell after just 12 days on the market, and houses in Alpharetta are selling for an average price of $555,000. Ranked under the top 25 places to live in the country by The Providence Group, Alpharetta boasts several large tech employers, tons of restaurants, shops, coffee houses, bars, and more.
